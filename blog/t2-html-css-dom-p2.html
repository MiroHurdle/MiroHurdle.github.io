Grid Based Design

Grid based design is the method of designing a web page pased on rows and columns. By breaking the page down into rows with columns of various widths in each row, it is easy to lay out a simple, organized, and easily accessible website. Generally the grid is broken down in to 12 columns because 12 is an even number that can easily be broken down into halves, thirds, quartes or even sixths. It gives you most flexibility while still being wide enough to read text or other important information at its smallest denomination. With a fixed number of columns in each row it is easy to know how much space is available and organize your page accordingly.

Responsive Webpages

Responsive webpages are increasingly important now that people are viewing online content using a number of devices with a wide variety of screen sizes. A responsive webpage is one that will adjust based on the size of the used window, so that you can view a full image without scrolling on a smartphone or a 27 inch desktop. This can take the form of resizing, hiding, or moving elements on the page so that it still looks good to the user no matter what device they are using. Responsive web design can be the difference between someone using your site or another better designed one with more functionality.

Semantic Structure

Semantic structure refers to the tags and syntax used in html. It is how your organize a webpage and decide what type of tag a given element should have. For instance often the title of the page would go under an h1 tag because it is bigger than the rest of the text, and it is one of the most general pieces of information on the page. The semantic structure dictates how the page looks and what elements appear where.
